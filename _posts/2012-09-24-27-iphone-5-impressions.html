In this week's episode of AppCorner we chat about our first impressions of the iPhone 5 - the taller screen, lighter weight, panorama camera mode, etc. We also chat to two of our developers, Ben and Alex, about their experience waiting in line overnight for the phone and coding an app while in line. Of course, we also recommend our picks (or deletions) for app of the week!

AppCorner is hosted by the team at Raizlabs. This week's podcast crew included Greg Raiz (@graiz), Ben Johnson (@scoober), Dave Counts (@davecounts), Jason Petralia (@jasonpetralia), Alex Rouse (@arrouse88) and Katie Tomezsko (@CTomezsko).

Apps and other cool things discussed in this weeks podcast include:

  • TalkTo - makes it easy to text any business for information, appointments, reservations or whatever you need, so you can avoid the hassle of phone calls.
    It’s as easy as texting a friend. Just type in the name of the store, restaurant, salon, hotel or any other business you want to contact and send your message. Then get on with your day! TalkTo will make sure your message is delivered to that business and that you receive a text response.
  • NOAA Hi-Def - Simple yet powerful app for viewing real-time animated weather radar images in vivid color on a highly responsive interactive map, with higher resolution images than any other radar app in the appstore.
  • Rachel Maddow - Be in the know. Stay connected to everything you love about the show – from the latest videos and photos to fan pages and the guest list. The Rachel Maddow Show app is one of the leading cable news apps available for the iPhone and iPod touch.
  • Linea - Designed for iPad and iPhone, Linea is the simple and stunning way to store, share and view all of your photos. Using Linea's unique patent pending MosaicView™, your images are automatically displayed in a Photo Line of memories perfect for instant editing, easy printing and effortless sharing with friends.
  • 360 Panorama - Take stunning 360 panoramas everywhere. 360 Panorama turns your iPhone, iPod Touch, or iPad into a full featured 360 degree panoramic camera. Easily capture immersive panoramas in seconds and instantly share your 360 view with the world. Simply pan the camera and watch as images are stitched seamlessly. 360 Panorama captures the scene in a way you never could with a traditional camera.
  • Fishballs - Fish Balls combines billiards style physics and colorful graphics into a game that is perfect to challenge your mind for a few minutes or a few hours. Share your scores on Facebook and Twitter and dare your friends to do better. Your goal is to shoot and then pop as many Fish Balls as possible. Each level gets progressively harder as the balls become harder to break. Take careful aim. The game ends if your ball bounces back across the line.
