In this week's episode of AppCorner we chat about Apple quietly resigning their claim that Macintosh products can't get viruses, the Google I/O conference and what we expect to see - Nexus Tablet reveals, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and the Google Glass project. We also discuss first impressions of Microsoft's Windows 8 phone - new features include Skype integration, turn-by-turn navigation and new homes screen, and select our app recommendations for the week (including an app rant).

AppCorner is hosted by the team at Raizlabs. This week's podcast crew included Bruno Germansderfer (@brunog), Andrew Tremblay (@drewlooksround) and Michelle Buchman (@tres_michelle).

Apps discussed in this weeks podcast include:

  • Podcasts - Podcasts app is the easiest way to discover, subscribe to, and play your favorite podcasts on your iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch. Explore hundreds of thousands of free audio and video podcasts from the Podcasts Catalog, and play the most popular podcasts, organized for you by topic, with the all-new Top Stations feature.
  • Meridian - Bring your maps into the 21st century. Meridian lets you easily create turn-by-turn directions to anywhere in your space, just like Google Maps, using your existing map images. Meridian uses GPS when available to tell visitors where they are, and even integrates with indoor location-sensing systems like Cisco’s MSE.
  • Temple Run: Brave - From Imangi, the makers of Temple Run, and Disney comes a new take on the most exhilarating app in the App Store. Join Merida from Disney/Pixar Brave as you run, slide, jump, turn and shoot your way across the wilds of Scotland on an endless running adventure.
