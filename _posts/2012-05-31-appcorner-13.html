In this week's episode of AppCorner we talk with one of our developers as he defends his Facebook stock purchase, discuss the possibility/speculation about a potential Facebook phone, iPhone 5 and WWDC announcement rumors, and share our new favorite Tumblr - Text from Xcode. Additionally, we have our recommendations for apps (and software) of the week as usual.

AppCorner is hosted by the team at Raizlabs. This week's podcast crew included Greg Raiz (@graiz), Dave Counts (@davecounts), Rich Stern (@richstern), Bruno Germansderfer (@brunog) and Michelle Buchman (@tres_michelle).

[display_podcast]

Apps and tech discussed in this episode include:

  • Leap Motion - a small iPod sized USB peripheral that creates a 3D interaction space of 8 cubic feet to precisely interact with and control software on your laptop or desktop computer.
  • Coda 2 - an update to the popular web editor for Mac with improved UI, text editing, file management, clips, mySQL and more.
  • Luminance - gives you pro photo editing capabilities in a simple and elegant interface. Effortlessly add effects to your photos, choose from many built-in presets, crop the area you want, and copy edits you've made from one photo and apply them to multiple photos.
  • Cut The Rope: Experiments - a follow-up to ZeptoLab's award-winning mobile game Cut the Rope, and features the familiar little monster Om Nom who is discovered by a mad (but not bad) scientist determined to study his candy-loving behavior through a series of experiments.
  • With Mitt - the app which lets you customize photos with a variety of Mitt-inspired artistic frames, add personalized messages, and then share with your friends via email, Facebook, or Twitter was in the news this week for misspelling America, creating a new internet meme.
