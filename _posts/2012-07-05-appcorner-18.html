In this week's episode of AppCorner we chat about Google I/O announcements, the Apple vs. Samsung's Galaxy Nexus S III, chat about the possible death of RIM and as always, we select our app recommendations for the week (including an app delete).

AppCorner is hosted by the team at Raizlabs. This week's podcast crew included Greg Raiz (@graiz), Craig Spitzkoff (@oofoot), Dave Counts (@davecounts) and Michelle Buchman (@tres_michelle).

[display_podcast]

Apps discussed in this weeks podcast include:

  • Google Chrome - Browse fast with Chrome, now available on your iPhone, iPod touch and iPad. Sign in to sync your personalized Chrome experience from your computer, and bring it with you anywhere you go.
  • Just Landed - Intelligently updates itself based on your current location and the latest flight and traffic information. Just go about your day and the app will let you know when it’s time to head for the airport.
  • Any.DO - Helps you remember everything you have to do. It's free, simple & fun. Millions of people use Any.DO to remember everything they have to do. With Any.DO you can easily capture all the things you want to do and make sure you get them done. It's free, simple, & fun.
  • Google+ - Share and keep up with people no matter where you are with the Google+ app. Join a hangout, post a photo, or see what friends are sharing while you’re on the go. Use Messenger to get everyone on the same page with fast, simple group chat.
  • CardioGraph - An application which measures your heart rate. You can save your results for future reference, keep track of multiple people with individual profiles, add notes and locations, and even print out your measurements for sharing or safe keeping.
  • Palm Booth - Takes a picture of your palm, displays it on screen, and then shows you exactly what the different parts of your hand really mean.
