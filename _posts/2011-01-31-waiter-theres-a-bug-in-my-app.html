Raizlabs mobilized testing recently with the launch of an iPhone and iPad app for uTest, the world's largest software testing marketplace. uTest currently utilizes Flash on their website making it inaccessible to users with iOS mobile devices.  With customers spanning six continents - and 30,000+ testers spanning the globe - creating an app for the world's most popular smartphone and tablet was critical to enabling uTest’s community to stay on top of complex and dynamic global testing projects.  Raizlabs worked with uTest to create a function-rich, eye-catching, easy-to-use version of their site to provide customers and testers access wherever and whenever they need it.

Customers can now manage-on-the-go by:

  • Keeping real-time tabs on their functional, load, and usability testing projects
  • Approving, rejecting and tracking reported defects and surveys from their iOS devices
  • Communicating directly with testers
  • Reviewing screenshots and video attachments that highlight defects

Testers can now test-on-the-go by:

  • Accepting invitations to uTest projects and submitting defects or surveys
  • Testing any app directly from their iPhones or iPads
  • Uploading defect screenshots and videos
  • Responding to inquiries from customers and other testers
  • Viewing their earnings from uTest projects

“Increasingly, retail, social and media apps have development and launch cycles that span 24 hours a day, around the globe,” said Doron Reuveni, uTest CEO. “Additionally, our customers and testers are increasingly reviewing and testing apps on evenings and weekends when they’re not in front of their computer. With uTest’s iPhone and iPad apps, our customers can stay on top of their mission-critical testing projects anytime, anywhere.”

Greg Raiz, founder of Raizlabs went on to say...."We're really excited to help uTest deliver a mobile testing platform. uTest recognizes the importance of mobile and having the tools available for users is just the start of a larger shift that we're seeing toward mobile testing, development and app deployment. "

The app is free and now available for download in the iTunes App Store. To download the uTest app, or for more information, visit http://apps.utest.com/ios.
